The Birthday Paradox

A counterintuitive probability problem showing how quickly shared birthdays become likely in a group.

The Question

How many people do you need in a room before there's a 50% chance that two people share the same birthday?

Most people guess around 183 (half of 365), but the actual answer is just 23 people!

Why It Works

Key Insight: We're not asking if someone shares YOUR birthday. We're asking if ANY two people share a birthday.

The Math: With 23 people, there are 253 possible pairs. Each pair has a 1/365 chance of matching.

Formula: P(match) = 1 - (365/365 × 364/365 × 363/365 × ... × 343/365)

Fun Facts:

  • • 50 people: 97% chance
  • • 70 people: 99.9% chance
  • • 367 people: 100% (Pigeonhole Principle)

Learn More

This paradox was first discussed in the context of probability theory and has practical applications in cryptography and hash collisions.
