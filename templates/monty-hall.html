Contrary

The Monty Hall Problem

A famous probability puzzle that demonstrates how our intuition can mislead us about statistics.

The Setup

You're on a game show with three doors behind one of the doors is a car (or any prize) behind the other two are goats.

After you pick a door the host opens another door revealing a goat (always a goat) Now you have a choice: stay with your original pick or switch to the other unopened door

Why Switch?

Initial Choice: When you first pick, you have a 1/3 chance of choosing the car and a 2/3 chance of choosing a goat.

Key Insight: If you initially picked a goat (2/3 probability), the host must reveal the other goat, leaving the car behind the remaining door.

The Math: Switching wins whenever your initial choice was wrong (2/3 of the time). Staying only wins if your initial choice was right (1/3 of the time).

Result: Switching doubles your chances of winning from 33% to 66%!!!!

This puzzle first appeared in a letter to American Statistician in 1975 and became famous through Marilyn vos Savant's column.
