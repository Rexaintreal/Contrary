The Paradox:

If you always travel half the remaining distance, you make infinite steps. So how do you ever reach your destination? The answer lies in infinite series convergence.

Contrary

Zeno's Paradox

An ancient philosophical problem about motion and infinity that challenges our understanding of space and time.

The Setup

Achilles and a tortoise have a race. The tortoise gets a head start. Zeno argues that Achilles can never overtake the tortoise.

Why? Because first Achilles must reach where the tortoise started. But by then, the tortoise has moved forward. Then Achilles must reach that new position, but the tortoise has moved again.

This continues infinitely, so Achilles never catches up. Yet we know he does catch up in reality.

The Resolution

Infinite Series: The paradox assumes infinite steps take infinite time. But mathematically, an infinite series can have a finite sum.

The Math: If Achilles travels 1/2, then 1/4, then 1/8 of the remaining distance, the sum is: 1/2 + 1/4 + 1/8 + 1/16... = 1

Modern Understanding: Calculus and the concept of limits resolve this. Infinite steps in space can occur in finite time.

Key Insight:

  • • Infinite steps don't mean infinite time
  • • Each step takes less time than the previous
  • • The total time converges to a finite value
